Company Dress Code Policy

The purpose of this Dress Code Policy is to ensure that all employees maintain a professional appearance while representing the company. Appropriate clothing creates a positive workplace environment and leaves a good impression on clients, customers, and visitors.

1. Objective

The company encourages employees to dress in a neat, clean, and professional manner. Employees should always maintain a respectful appearance suitable for their work responsibilities.

2. General Guidelines

  • Wear clean and well-maintained clothing.
  • Maintain personal hygiene.
  • Avoid clothing with offensive text or images.
  • Wear your company ID card while on company premises.
  • Dress appropriately for meetings and presentations.

3. Office Dress Standards

Day Dress Code
Monday - Thursday Business Casual
Friday Smart Casual
Client Meetings Formal Business Attire

4. Acceptable Clothing

  • Formal shirts and trousers
  • Business suits
  • Collared T-shirts
  • Formal shoes
  • Clean jeans on casual Fridays
  • Professional dresses and blazers

5. Unacceptable Clothing

  • Ripped jeans
  • Shorts
  • Sleeveless shirts
  • Slippers and flip-flops
  • Clothing with offensive slogans
  • Beachwear

6. Grooming Standards

Employees should maintain good personal hygiene and grooming. Hair should be clean and neatly maintained. Facial hair should be well groomed.

  • Maintain professional hairstyle.
  • Use mild perfumes only.
  • Keep nails clean.
  • Maintain overall cleanliness.

7. Safety Requirements

Employees working in laboratories, manufacturing units, or technical areas must wear the required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE).

  • Safety shoes
  • Helmets
  • Gloves
  • Protective jackets
  • Safety goggles

8. Dress Code During Events

Employees participating in company events may wear company-branded T-shirts or event-specific uniforms as instructed by management.

9. Religious and Cultural Dress

The company respects all religious and cultural practices. Employees may wear appropriate traditional attire provided it is safe and suitable for the workplace.

10. Policy Violations

Employees not following the dress code may receive a verbal reminder. Repeated violations may lead to disciplinary action according to company policy.

11. Responsibilities

  • Employees should follow the dress code daily.
  • Managers should ensure compliance within their teams.
  • HR will address policy-related concerns.

12. Conclusion

Following the company dress code promotes professionalism, improves workplace culture, and strengthens the company's brand image. Employees are expected to follow these guidelines while carrying out their daily responsibilities.
