Company Office Timing Policy

The Office Timing Policy establishes standard working hours, attendance expectations, break schedules, and punctuality guidelines for all employees. Following office timings ensures smooth business operations, teamwork, and efficient customer service.

1. Purpose

The objective of this policy is to define official office hours, promote discipline, and ensure employees complete their daily responsibilities within scheduled working hours.

2. Standard Office Hours

Category Office Timing
Working Days Monday to Friday
Office Hours 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM
Lunch Break 1:00 PM – 2:00 PM
Tea Break 15 Minutes (Morning & Afternoon)
Weekly Off Saturday & Sunday

3. Punctuality

Employees are expected to report to work on time every working day. Punctuality reflects professionalism and helps maintain team productivity.

  • Arrive before the official start time.
  • Complete attendance immediately after arrival.
  • Inform your manager if you expect to be late.
  • Repeated lateness may result in disciplinary action.

4. Attendance Recording

  • Attendance must be marked daily.
  • Use the company's attendance system.
  • Proxy attendance is strictly prohibited.
  • Attendance records are used during payroll processing.

5. Flexible Working Hours

Certain departments may be allowed flexible working hours based on project requirements and manager approval. Employees must still complete their required working hours each day.

6. Break Policy

  • Lunch Break – 1 Hour
  • Morning Tea Break – 15 Minutes
  • Afternoon Tea Break – 15 Minutes
  • Breaks should not affect work commitments.

7. Overtime

Employees may be required to work beyond regular office hours during urgent business situations. Overtime must be approved by the reporting manager.

  • Advance approval is required.
  • Overtime is recorded by HR.
  • Compensation follows company policy.

8. Remote Work Timing

Employees working remotely must follow the same office timings unless otherwise approved by their manager.

  • Stay available during office hours.
  • Attend virtual meetings on time.
  • Respond promptly to work communications.

9. Early Departure

Employees wishing to leave early must obtain prior approval from their reporting manager. Unauthorized early departures may affect attendance records.

10. Absence Reporting

  • Inform your manager before office hours whenever possible.
  • Submit leave requests through the HR system.
  • Emergency absences should be reported immediately.

11. Employee Responsibilities

  • Follow office timings every day.
  • Maintain punctuality.
  • Complete assigned work during office hours.
  • Respect meeting schedules.
  • Follow attendance procedures.

12. Manager Responsibilities

  • Monitor team attendance.
  • Approve leave and overtime requests.
  • Ensure business continuity.
  • Address attendance concerns fairly.

13. Policy Violations

Repeated late arrivals, unauthorized absences, failure to record attendance, or leaving the workplace without permission may lead to disciplinary action, salary deductions, or other corrective measures as per company policy.

14. Policy Review

The company reserves the right to update this Office Timing Policy based on business requirements, operational needs, or changes in applicable laws and regulations.
