Company Attendance Policy

The Attendance Policy establishes guidelines for employee attendance, punctuality, and working hours. Regular attendance is essential for maintaining productivity, teamwork, and efficient business operations. Every employee is expected to follow the attendance rules outlined in this policy.

1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to ensure employees maintain regular attendance, arrive on time, and inform management in case of absence or delay.

2. Office Working Hours

Category Timing
Working Days Monday to Friday
Office Hours 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM
Lunch Break 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM
Weekly Off Saturday & Sunday

3. Attendance Recording

Employees must record their attendance every working day using the company's approved attendance system.

  • Attendance must be marked daily.
  • Employees should mark attendance immediately after arrival.
  • Proxy attendance is strictly prohibited.
  • Attendance records are maintained by the HR Department.

4. Punctuality

  • Employees should report to work on time.
  • Late arrivals should be informed to the reporting manager.
  • Repeated late coming may result in disciplinary action.
  • Habitual lateness may affect performance evaluation.

5. Absence Reporting

Employees who are unable to attend work must inform their reporting manager as early as possible and submit a leave request through the HR system.

  • Inform the manager before office hours whenever possible.
  • Emergency leave should be communicated immediately.
  • Medical leave may require supporting documents.

6. Late Arrival Policy

Late Arrival Action
Up to 15 Minutes Allowed Occasionally
More than 15 Minutes Manager Review Required
Repeated Late Arrivals Warning or Salary Deduction

7. Early Departure

Employees wishing to leave the office before the official closing time must obtain prior approval from their reporting manager.

  • Manager approval is mandatory.
  • Unauthorized early departure may affect attendance records.
  • Emergency situations will be handled individually.

8. Work From Home Attendance

Employees working remotely must maintain attendance and remain available during official working hours.

  • Login at the scheduled time.
  • Attend online meetings.
  • Submit daily work updates.
  • Respond to official communications promptly.

9. Leave and Attendance

Approved leave will not be treated as absence. Employees should apply for leave in advance whenever possible.

  • Casual Leave
  • Sick Leave
  • Earned Leave
  • Maternity / Paternity Leave
  • Leave Without Pay (if applicable)

10. Attendance During Training

Employees attending official training programs, workshops, or seminars are considered present if participation is approved by the company.

11. Employee Responsibilities

  • Maintain regular attendance.
  • Report absences promptly.
  • Follow office timing.
  • Record attendance honestly.
  • Complete assigned work within office hours.

12. Manager Responsibilities

  • Monitor employee attendance.
  • Approve leave requests fairly.
  • Address attendance concerns promptly.
  • Maintain accurate attendance records.

13. Policy Violations

Repeated absenteeism, unauthorized leave, proxy attendance, or failure to follow attendance procedures may result in disciplinary action, salary deductions, performance warnings, or other corrective measures according to company policy.

14. Attendance Best Practices

  • Plan leave in advance.
  • Reach the office before reporting time.
  • Keep your manager informed.
  • Maintain professionalism and punctuality.
  • Follow all HR attendance guidelines.

15. Policy Review

The company reserves the right to modify this Attendance Policy at any time to meet business requirements, legal obligations, or operational needs. Employees will be notified of significant updates through official communication channels.
