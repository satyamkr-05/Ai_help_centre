Company Leave Policy

The Leave Policy is designed to help employees maintain a healthy work-life balance while ensuring business continuity. Employees are encouraged to plan their leave responsibly and follow the leave approval process.

1. Purpose

This policy explains the different types of leave available to employees, eligibility criteria, approval procedures, and employee responsibilities.

2. Leave Categories

Leave Type Days Per Year
Casual Leave (CL) 12 Days
Sick Leave (SL) 10 Days
Earned Leave (EL) 15 Days
Maternity Leave As per Government Rules
Paternity Leave 5 Days
Bereavement Leave 3 Days

3. Casual Leave

  • Employees are entitled to 12 casual leaves per year.
  • Leave should be applied in advance whenever possible.
  • Unused casual leave cannot be carried forward.

4. Sick Leave

  • Employees receive 10 sick leaves annually.
  • A medical certificate may be required for leave exceeding two days.
  • Managers should be informed as soon as possible.

5. Earned Leave

Earned leave is accumulated based on company policy and may be carried forward according to HR guidelines.

  • Advance approval is required.
  • Leave encashment is subject to company policy.

6. Maternity & Paternity Leave

Eligible employees are entitled to maternity and paternity leave as per applicable labor laws and company regulations.

7. Public Holidays

Employees are entitled to company-declared public holidays. The holiday calendar is shared by the HR Department at the beginning of every year.

8. Leave Application Process

  1. Submit leave request through the HR Portal.
  2. Inform your reporting manager.
  3. Wait for manager approval.
  4. Receive confirmation from HR.

9. Leave Approval Guidelines

  • Business requirements will be considered before approval.
  • Emergency leave should be reported immediately.
  • Long-term leave requires additional approvals.

10. Unauthorized Absence

Failure to report to work without prior approval or valid reason may result in salary deduction and disciplinary action.

11. Leave During Probation

  • Leave availability during probation is limited.
  • Manager approval is mandatory.
  • Excess leave may be treated as Leave Without Pay (LWP).

12. Leave Without Pay (LWP)

Employees who exhaust all available leave may request Leave Without Pay. Approval depends on management discretion.

13. Employee Responsibilities

  • Plan leave responsibly.
  • Complete pending work before going on leave.
  • Keep the reporting manager informed.
  • Return to work on the approved date.

14. Manager Responsibilities

  • Review leave requests fairly.
  • Ensure business continuity.
  • Maintain leave records.

15. Policy Review

The HR Department reserves the right to modify this Leave Policy whenever required to meet business or legal requirements.
