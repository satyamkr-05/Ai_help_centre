Company Work From Home Policy

The Work From Home (WFH) Policy provides employees with the flexibility to work remotely while maintaining productivity, collaboration, and information security. Employees working from home are expected to perform their duties with the same professionalism and commitment as they would in the office.

1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to define the eligibility, responsibilities, communication standards, and security requirements for employees working remotely.

2. Eligibility

  • Work From Home is available only for eligible employees.
  • Manager approval is mandatory before working remotely.
  • Some departments may require regular office attendance.
  • Business requirements always take priority.

3. Standard WFH Schedule

Item Policy
Maximum WFH Days 2 Days Per Week
Working Hours 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM
Lunch Break 1 Hour
Approval Required Yes

4. Working Hours

Employees must remain available during official working hours and respond to emails, messages, and meeting invitations promptly.

  • Login on time.
  • Attend all scheduled meetings.
  • Maintain regular communication with the team.
  • Submit assigned work within deadlines.

5. Communication Guidelines

Employees should actively communicate with managers and team members throughout the workday.

  • Check email regularly.
  • Keep status updated on communication tools.
  • Respond to urgent messages promptly.
  • Join virtual meetings with camera when required.

6. Productivity Expectations

  • Complete assigned tasks on time.
  • Maintain quality standards.
  • Provide daily work updates.
  • Participate in team discussions.
  • Meet project deadlines.

7. Equipment and Internet

Employees are responsible for maintaining a stable internet connection and using company-approved devices whenever possible.

  • Laptop/Desktop
  • Reliable Internet Connection
  • Headset for Online Meetings
  • Power Backup (Recommended)

8. Information Security

Employees must protect company data while working remotely.

  • Use strong passwords.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • Never share confidential information.
  • Use company VPN whenever required.
  • Lock your computer when away.

9. Data Privacy

Confidential company information must never be accessed using public computers or unsecured Wi-Fi networks. Employees should avoid discussing confidential business matters in public places.

10. Performance Monitoring

Managers may review employee productivity based on completed tasks, attendance, communication, and project delivery rather than physical presence.

11. Health and Safety

  • Maintain a comfortable workspace.
  • Take short breaks during work.
  • Follow ergonomic seating practices.
  • Ensure adequate lighting.

12. Employee Responsibilities

  • Maintain regular office hours.
  • Protect company equipment.
  • Keep work information confidential.
  • Report technical issues immediately.
  • Follow all company policies.

13. Manager Responsibilities

  • Approve WFH requests fairly.
  • Monitor work progress.
  • Provide guidance and support.
  • Ensure team collaboration.

14. Policy Violations

Failure to comply with this policy, including repeated unavailability during working hours, misuse of company equipment, or violation of information security rules, may result in suspension of Work From Home privileges or disciplinary action.

15. Policy Review

The company reserves the right to modify or discontinue the Work From Home Policy based on business needs, operational requirements, or changes in applicable laws.
