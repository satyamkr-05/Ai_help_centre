Company Payroll Policy

The Payroll Policy defines the company's salary payment process, employee compensation structure, deductions, reimbursements, and payroll administration. The objective of this policy is to ensure employees are paid accurately, fairly, and on time while complying with applicable labor and tax laws.

1. Purpose

This policy explains how salaries are calculated, processed, approved, and credited to employees. It also outlines statutory deductions, overtime, bonuses, and reimbursement procedures.

2. Payroll Cycle

Item Details
Payroll Frequency Monthly
Salary Credit Date Last Working Day of Every Month
Payment Method Direct Bank Transfer
Salary Slip Available on Employee Portal

3. Salary Structure

Employee salary generally consists of the following components:

  • Basic Salary
  • House Rent Allowance (HRA)
  • Special Allowance
  • Transport Allowance (if applicable)
  • Medical Allowance
  • Performance Incentives
  • Annual Bonus (where applicable)

4. Salary Deductions

The following deductions may apply depending on company policy and legal requirements.

  • Provident Fund (PF)
  • Professional Tax
  • Income Tax (TDS)
  • Employee State Insurance (ESI)
  • Leave Without Pay (LWP)
  • Other authorized deductions

5. Attendance and Payroll

Payroll processing is based on approved attendance records submitted before the monthly payroll cutoff date. Incorrect attendance records may affect salary calculations.

  • Late attendance may impact payroll according to company rules.
  • Unauthorized absences may result in salary deductions.
  • Approved leave will not affect salary.

6. Overtime Policy

Eligible employees may receive overtime compensation based on prior management approval and applicable company rules.

  • Overtime must be approved in advance.
  • HR verifies overtime records before payment.
  • Overtime rates follow company policy.

7. Bonus and Incentives

Employees may receive performance-based bonuses and incentives depending on their individual performance, company profitability, and management approval.

8. Expense Reimbursement

Employees may claim reimbursement for approved business expenses by submitting valid bills and supporting documents.

  • Travel Expenses
  • Accommodation
  • Client Meetings
  • Training Programs
  • Official Purchases

9. Payroll Corrections

Employees who notice any discrepancy in their salary should immediately contact the HR or Payroll Department. Verified errors will be corrected in the next payroll cycle or through a separate adjustment if required.

10. Confidentiality

Salary information is confidential. Employees must not disclose their salary details or access another employee's payroll information without authorization.

11. Employee Responsibilities

  • Maintain accurate bank account details.
  • Submit attendance regularly.
  • Report payroll discrepancies promptly.
  • Keep tax declarations updated.

12. HR & Payroll Responsibilities

  • Process payroll accurately and on time.
  • Maintain payroll records securely.
  • Ensure statutory compliance.
  • Provide salary slips every month.

13. Payroll Timeline

Activity Timeline
Attendance Submission 25th of Every Month
Payroll Processing 26th–29th
Salary Credit Last Working Day
Salary Slip Release Within 2 Days After Salary Credit

14. Policy Review

The company reserves the right to amend or update this Payroll Policy whenever required due to changes in business operations, statutory regulations, or organizational requirements.
